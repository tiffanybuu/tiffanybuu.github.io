Travels: Gimmelwald, Switzerland

Date Visited: July 2018

During my study abroad endeavors in Madrid, I booked a flight to Gimmelwald, Switzerland, a small farming village in the midst of the Swiss Alps. We first stopped in Geneva, took two trains, switched to a bus, and finally took a cable car to the village. With a population of only 130, it was a much needed break from the hustle and bustle of city-life and tourists I was accustomed to seeing on the streets of Madrid. I stayed at the Mountain Hostel, where guests and the workers alike were very friendly and down to earth. My friends and I decided to hike to the top of a small peak, called Bryndl. The hike took around three hours to go up and was mostly uphill, but the views were absolutely spectacular. I highly recommend visiting this lovely village, it is probably my favorite travel destinations I have been to! (More photos coming soon!)
