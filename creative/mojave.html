Travels: Mojave, CA

Date Visited: January 2019

On a rather spontaneous trip to Las Vegas, I took a day trip to Mojave, California to visit the Mojave National Preserve. The Preserve is teeming with wildlife, an assortment of cinder cone volcanos, beds of wildflowers, a variety of uniquely shaped Joshua Trees, and vast sand dunes. Once we drove to the Kelso Dunes, I was determined to climb to the top. With some encouraging words to my father, we decided to climb to the top of one of the peaks (we had no map nor plan in mind, and it was mostly my adrenaline leading the way). It took around 1.5 hours (would have taken less if I didn't stop for photos every ten steps), and the view at the top was stunning. We had reached the top at around 4:30 pm, and the sunset hours were just around the corner. I really felt like I was at the top of the world, and with no other tourists around at the time (with the exception of two other hikers at the other peak, whom you can spot in the photo), I took advantage of the spectacular views and the fresh air. While we did get lost on the way down (ended up a mile away from our car), it was worth it, and the sunset that covered the sky consisted of the most spectacular hues of reds and purples against the silhouettes of the joshua trees (more photos to come)..
